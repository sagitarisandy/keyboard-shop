Build Guide: First Custom Keyboard

Parts Checklist

  • Case + plate + PCB (hot-swap recommended)
  • Switches (70–90 pcs depending on layout)
  • Keycaps set (profile of your choice)
  • Stabilizers (pre-clipped or screw-in)
  • Cable (USB-C preferred)

Assembly Steps

  • Install stabilizers on the PCB and test fit.
  • Place the plate over PCB then insert switches to lock alignment.
  • Mount the assembly into the case (gasket or screw as needed).
  • Connect via USB and test keys using a key tester site.
  • Install keycaps and do a final functional check.

Tip: Test switches in batches to avoid chasing issues later.

Sound Tuning: Quick Wins

Tape & Foam Mods

  • Apply 1–3 layers of painter's tape to PCB back.
  • Add case foam to reduce hollowness and reverb.
  • Use plate foam to tame ping and improve feel.

Switch & Stabilizer Lube

  • Lightly lube stems and rails for linears/tactiles.
  • Grease stabilizer wires to remove rattle.
  • Avoid over-lubing clickies to retain their character.

Frequently Asked Questions

Hot-swap vs Solder?

Hot-swap is beginner-friendly and fast. Soldered PCBs can be cheaper and more durable but require tools and practice.

Best layout for work?

65% or 75% balances compactness with arrow keys and functions. TKL offers full navigation if you need it.
